Understand the Technologies Changing the Digital World


Change Is Already Here
The digital revolution started since around 1980's, with the mass use of computers, digitation and automation, and the creation of Internet. Today, there are new powerful technologies making their mark on the digital world. Let's explore how artificial intelligence (AI), blockchain, computational technologies, and virtual and augmented reality are transforming the digital world.

Technologies Changing the Digital World
    -Artificial intelligence (AI)
    -Blockchain applications
    -New computational technologies
    -Virtual and augmented reality

Artificial Intelligence (AI)


Contrary to the ominous portrayals in fictional movies, the reality of AI today is far from evil robots taking over. AI operates behind the scenes, powering various applications like autonomous vehicles, voice assistants, and online shopping recommendations. Examples include the autocomplete feature on your phone, personalized website suggestions, and facial recognition in photos—all made possible by AI. Current AI systems excel at tasks such as pattern recognition, information synthesis, drawing conclusions, and forecasting, performing these tasks faster and on a larger scale than humans.

Blockchain Applications

Blockchain is a method of recording and sharing data where everyone sees the same information. It ensures data integrity by making any changes visible to everyone, along with the identity of the person making the change. This technology builds trust between parties, even if they are anonymous. Technically, blockchain is a shared, programmable, decentralized, and cryptographically secure ledger for storing digital objects and transactions. No single user or entity controls blockchain applications, allowing anyone to scrutinize them. Bitcoin serves as an example, using blockchain for a public ledger of digital currency transactions. In addition to digital currency, blockchain technology can handle any transaction that is expressible as computer code. Possible scenarios include:

-Insurance claims
-Votes
-Real estate titles
-Driver’s licenses

Someday, voting and national identities can perhaps be supported through blockchain technologies. Blockchain can possibly even be used to trace where your products came from and that they are not fakes.

New Computational Technologies

Advancements in material science and physics are making our computers smarter and faster. Quantum computing, a new form of computing, is a game-changer, offering the potential for systems millions of times more powerful than current ones.
In simple terms, traditional computers use 1s and 0s to store information. Quantum computers, on the other hand, use quantum bits or qubits, which can represent both 0 and 1 simultaneously. This unique capability allows quantum computers to perform calculations at a speed millions of times faster than regular computers.
The impact of quantum computing extends to supercharging AI, accelerating pharmaceutical and material discoveries, and rapidly processing highly complex data models.

Virtual and Augmented Reality

Virtual Reality (VR) offers immersive digital experiences, simulating various scenarios like touring ancient Rome or participating in movies about evil robots. Currently requiring bulky and expensive headsets, VR technology is expected to become smaller and more affordable over time. In contrast, Augmented Reality (AR) merges the digital and physical worlds. AR-enabled devices provide useful information relevant to your location, enhancing real-world experiences. Examples include architects experimenting with building designs or doctors overlaying MRI data during surgeries. Both AR and VR have the potential to transform our interaction with computers in the coming decades, with people likely incorporating devices that blend the virtual and physical worlds into their daily lives.

Overall

Each of these technological advances is paving the way for significant changes in our personal and work lives. But it’s the combination of these technologies that provides the foundation for the Fourth Industrial Revolution. For example:

-Robots take advantage of AI.
-Small and efficient batteries power our phones.
-Connected arrays of sensors play a crucial role in our daily lives by notifying us when our car requires servicing or when all the lights are on at home.
-Scientists can use powerful computation capabilities, 3D printing, and biotechnology to engineer new materials.

The technologies of the Fourth Industrial Revolution hold great promise for a brighter future, but if not handled carefully, they could also destabilize society. Similar to past industrial revolutions, these advancements bring both positive and negative impacts. There are concerns about the potential misuse of AI and genetic engineering, which could have undesirable consequences if not aligned with the common good. Moreover, there's a risk that the benefits of these technologies may not be equally accessible to all, excluding certain global citizens.
To prevent that, we need to work together and collaborate to shape the paths of Fourth Industrial Revolution technologies to minimize risks and enhance the well-being of all.
